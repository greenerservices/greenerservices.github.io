9. Work as greener teams

The impacts of the team, whether in-house or from suppliers, and the way they work through the lifecycle phases to design and deliver are one of the ‘embodied’ impacts of a service. If set up to work in green ways, teams consume less energy and other resources during the design and development of the service. Teams may be in-house or suppliers.

9a. Create a low-carbon non-functional requirement (NFR) for the project

Lifecycle phases

Alpha Beta

Actions

(i) Create the Non-functional Requirement

Sustainability should be regarded as a factor that has influence over all other project requirements, with the NFR covering the whole lifecycle of the service (from production through to use and then end-of-life) and setting metrics so success can be measured.

9b. Choose the right collaborative practices for the team

Actions

(i) Choosing collaboration tools and document sharing

The right collaboration tools for particular tasks, such as digital whiteboard software for workshops and diagramming, can save teams time and effort, in turn reducing energy consumption. Share documents via use of platforms synchronised with a shared server rather than email

(ii) Have a plan in place for team travel and in-person meetings

Business travel usually has a very signficant environmental footprint. Adopt a green approach to meetings, and put tech packages and policies in place to mean business travel is only used when delivering absolute benefit.

(iii) Approaching meetings

Set up purposeful meetings, with a clear agenda and actions to takeaway. If the meetings are online, turn off video after the initial intros have been made.

Measurement

There are a number of tools that can help us estimate a team's or individual's environmental footprint:

  • My Impact is an open source tool that allows a ‘professional’ to estimate their personal impacts from equipment, video calls, cloud storage, emails and business travel.

  • UK Gov Emission Conversion Factors can be used to calculate the carbon footprint of a vast range of different activities

  • Travel impact calculator, LNER

  • Transport calculator, World Land Trust
